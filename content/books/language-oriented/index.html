The Language-Oriented Approach to API Development

How do organizations get their teams to build consistent, quality APIs? And how do they do this without affecting their teams’ autonomy and speed of delivery? It’s not an easy task.

The design-first approach with OpenAPI is too challenging. You need education programs to help people learn how to design with OpenAPI. You need pick good API styles, standards, and practices. You need tooling to help ensure people follow those styles, standards, and practices. And even after all this, there are more challenges to make sure the design matches the implementation.

This book is about a different approach to API design and development. The language-oriented approach puts the emphasis on the language your organization uses to design API designs. It encourages people to create DSLs tailored to their organization's styles, standards, and practices. The goal of this approach is to lower the barrier for people to design APIs the right way, and to create a design process that include more people than the developers.
